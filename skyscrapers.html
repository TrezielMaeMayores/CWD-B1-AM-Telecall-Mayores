Skyscrapers by T.M. MAYORES

face Treziel Mae Mayores|date_rangeNovember 1, 2022|draftsPoetry|insert_commentNo Comments
  • POEMarrow_drop_down

    Life is like a series of doors, You open one after another.

    Sometimes one can be easy to open As a puff of breath, the door would creak release.

    Sometimes it can be hard to push ajar, Heavy metal, crooked knobs and rusty hinges

    It’s exhausting but you can’t go back, You’ll see series of opened doors behind That you fought hard to get pass.

    You’ll realize thought it’s hard to crack, None of these doors were locked.

  • POET's BIOarrow_drop_down
    Treziel Mae Mayores is a student from the Philippines who likes getting lost in her thoughts. She spends most of her time writing on her blog called Ramblification while being a cat mom. She loves the rainy season but she treats the sun as her muse.
